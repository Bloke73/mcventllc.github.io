Spirit & Product Knowledge
Bar 3 written-test material — know this cold.

Fermentation

The biotransformation of organic substances via enzymes from yeast and bacteria. Yeast cells eat the sugars in your mash and excrete ethanol and CO₂.

Raw Organic Material (sugars) + Yeast & Enzymes + Water
= CO₂ (by-product) + Heat + Ethanol

Distillation

The process by which ethanol in a fermented substance is extracted and concentrated through successive heating and cooling. Many methods exist.

Distillate — the liquid product of distillation. Distillates categorized as alcohol beverages are called Spirits, separated into three parts: Heads, Hearts, and Tails.

Vodka

Colorless, odorless, tasteless neutral grain spirit from continuous stills, distilled to very high purity. Off the still it's 95–97% ethanol, then filtered through charcoal or silver and diluted to 35–50% ABV (80 proof).

Traditional mash: rye, wheat, potatoes, molasses from sugar beets. Today, any starchy or sugary plant.

Gin

Flavored neutral grain spirit, made via distillation and sometimes infusion or compounding. Flavored with juniper berries and botanicals.

London DryOriginally London, now made anywhere. No added sweeteners — juniper forward.
GeneverNotes of rye, maize, barley. Most popular in Holland & Belgium.
Old Tom"Cordial" style — masks rough notes with lemon or anise, or sweetened with licorice/sugar.
Sloe GinBritish, infused with blackthorn berries, sweetened with sugar. Purple in color.
Navy StrengthJoked to ignite near gunpowder if spilled on a naval ship. ~57% ABV.

Rum

Any distilled alcoholic beverage made from sugar cane or molasses. High sucrose content makes it ideal for alcohol production. Made worldwide, wherever sugarcane grows.

Mezcal

Made from roasting the heart of a piña (agave) plant to use as mash. Must now be made in one of nine Mexican states: Oaxaca, Durango, Guerrero, San Luis Potosí, Zacatecas, Tamaulipas, Guanajuato, Michoacán, Puebla.

Tequila

A regionally defined type of mezcal made through double distillation of 100% Blue Agave (Weber varietal). Named for the city of Tequila in Jalisco.

Can only be produced in Jalisco plus nearby municipalities: Michoacán, Nayarit & Tamaulipas.

Whisky / Whiskey

No global legal definition, but agreed to be a cereal-grain based, non-neutral grain spirit generally aged in wood barrels. Common grains: corn, barley, wheat, rye.

BourbonMust be made in the U.S., ≥51% corn (usually 5–15% barley, rest rye or wheat). Off-still ≤62.5% ABV, cut to 40–80% in bottle. Aged min. 2 years in charred new American white oak.
Canadian WhiskyDistilled in Canada. Aged in new or used American oak, min. 3 years. Bottled at 40% ABV or higher.
Irish Whiskey7 styles: Single Pot Still (triple distilled, barley malt + unmalted barley), Single Malt (triple/double distilled, malted barley), Grain Whisky (continuous still, mixed grains), Blended Whisky (a blend of the above).
Scotch WhiskyMatured in one Scottish warehouse, oak casks ≤700L, min. 3 years. Traditionally one water source + malted barley. Peat fire drying gives the smoky taste.
Japanese WhiskyYoungest whisky-making country — pioneered by Suntory & Nikka, founders trained in Scotland. More aromatic, lighter body than Scotch. Longer fermentation, ultra-clear wort, often aged in Japanese oak.
Marketing knowledge: Bar 3 bartenders should be able to briefly explain what Peak offers and the differences between bar packages at marketing events — but never quote or talk pricing.
Beer & Wine Styles
Bar 2 written-test material.

Beer

LagerBeer produced from bottom-fermenting yeast, distinguished from ales (top-fermented).
Pale AleFloral, fruity, citrus-like, piney, resinous American hops. Medium-bodied, low-medium caramel, toasted maltiness.
AmberMedium-high malt character, medium-low caramel from roasted crystal malts. American-hop notes of citrus, fruit, pine balance the malt sweetness.
IPAMade with extra hops. Ranges from intensely bitter to strong citrus.
StoutWarm-fermented ale style, dark color (opaque deep brown/black), distinct roasted note.

White Wine

Pinot GrisLess aromatic than Sauvignon Blanc, drier than Chardonnay. Full-bodied, fresh, spicy, notes of tropical/stone fruit or citrus. Riper grapes = less acidic; typically medium–medium-high acid.
Sauvignon BlancBright acidity, tart apple & kiwi. Cooler climates → grassy/green flavors; warmer climates → more tropical.
ChardonnayMalolactic fermentation gives buttery notes. Big difference between oaked and unoaked.

Red Wine

Pinot NoirJuicy acidity, bright red fruits. Pale-medium color, high acidity, fine soft tannins.
GarnachaDry, semi-sweet, or sweet styles. Raspberry, black cherry, strawberry; star anise, cinnamon, black pepper if oak-aged.
Cabernet SauvignonDark fruit — blackcurrant (cassis), black cherry, blackberry — with green bell pepper, spice, tobacco, wood, vanilla.
MalbecDry, full-bodied, dark fruit nose — blackberry, red plum. Jammy, with vanilla, tobacco, dark chocolate, oak notes. Medium acid, moderate tannins.
Build, Glass & Method Rules
The logic behind every spec — use this when a recipe doesn't say the method.
  • Drinks with juices are typically shaken.
  • Drinks that are spirit forward are stirred.
  • Built drinks are made in the glass they're served in, sometimes with a quick stir to incorporate juices/spirits.
  • Coupe glasses are for drinks served up (not on ice).
  • Drinks in a rocks glass are served over ice. A guest can ask for their "up" drink in a rocks glass instead.
  • Spritz drinks are typically served in a wine glass with ice.
Mocktail rule: Mocktails are 100% alcohol-free (0% ABV). You cannot use bitters in a mocktail — bitters are normally made with a neutral grain spirit base (like heavy-proof vodka). A mocktail = no bitters. Top mocktails with a soda of any flavor as a good finishing technique.
Bar 3 Practical Note
  • The Bar 3 practical is 5 drinks in 5 minutes, and can pull from both the Bar 2 and Bar 3 recipe lists — don't drop the Bar 2 classics once you move up.
  • Egg white drinks: dry shake first (no ice), then add ice and shake again — or reverse shake — then double fine strain.
  • Specialty drinks (marked S* in the class list) won't be at every event unless requested on the client's contract/menu.
